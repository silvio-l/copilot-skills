# Headline Formula: Verb + Benefit

## The Core Formula

Every app store screenshot headline follows one pattern:

```
[STRONG ACTION VERB] + [USER BENEFIT]
```

The verb grabs attention. The benefit sells the download.

## The 3 Questions (Ask Before Writing ANY Headline)

Before generating headlines, answer these three questions. They are the foundation
that makes headlines write themselves:

### 1. Who is the user?
Be specific. "Everyone" is not an answer.
- ❌ "People who want to manage money"
- ✅ "Young professionals who want to stop living paycheck to paycheck"
- ✅ "Parents who need to plan family expenses without spreadsheets"

### 2. What is the #1 reason they'd download this app?
One sentence. If you need two sentences, you haven't found it yet.
- ❌ "It has budgeting, savings goals, and expense tracking"
- ✅ "To see exactly how much money they have left to spend this month"

### 3. What transformation does this app deliver?
This is the secret weapon. Don't describe what the app DOES — describe what
the user BECOMES after using it.

| App Type | Feature (DON'T) | Transformation (DO) |
|----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Habit tracker | Tracks your daily habits | Makes you better every single day |
| Recipe app | Shows you recipes | Makes cooking at home effortless |
| Budget planner | Plans income and expenses | Shows you exactly what's left |
| Fitness app | Logs your workouts | Gets you in the best shape of your life |
| Language app | Has flashcard quizzes | Makes you fluent in months, not years |
| Note app | Stores your notes | Captures every idea before it's gone |
| Sleep tracker | Records sleep data | Helps you wake up refreshed every morning |
| Task manager | Creates to-do lists | Gets everything out of your head |

## The Action Verb Library

Choose verbs that are:
- **Active** — the user is doing something
- **Powerful** — they feel in control
- **Specific** — they know exactly what happens

### Tier 1: High-Impact Verbs (use for key screens)
Track, Plan, Reach, Boost, Master, Build, Save, Control, Achieve, Unlock

### Tier 2: Supporting Verbs (use for secondary screens)
Find, Learn, Create, Manage, Monitor, Discover, Simplify, Organize, Capture, Review

### Tier 3: Emotional Verbs (use for hero/closing screens)
Transform, Conquer, Crush, Dominate, Elevate, Empower, Thrive, Flourish

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

### ❌ Feature Listing
```
Screen 1: "Home Screen"
Screen 2: "Calendar View"
Screen 3: "Settings Page"
Screen 4: "Graph Feature"
```
This is a tour, not a pitch. Nobody cares about your settings page.

### ❌ Too Much Text
```
"Track your daily expenses, income, and savings goals
with our comprehensive financial planning tool that
supports multiple currencies and categories"
```
Nobody reads 3 lines on an app store screenshot. One line. Two max.

### ❌ No Verb
```
"Beautiful Dashboard"
"Smart Analytics"
"Powerful Features"
```
These are adjective + noun. They don't tell the user what to DO.
"Analyze your data" → verb + benefit.

### ❌ Vague Benefits
```
"The best app for you"
"Everything you need"
"All-in-one solution"
```
These could be for ANY app. Be specific. What exactly does the user get?

## Headline Writing Process

1. **Start with the transformation.** Write it as a full sentence.
   "This app makes planning your finances feel easy and motivating."

2. **Extract the verb.** What's the core action? → "Plan"

3. **Extract the benefit.** What does the user gain? → "your finances, easily"

4. **Compress to 5-8 words.** "Plan your finances with ease" or
   "Plan your month at a glance"

5. **Add emphasis.** Wrap 1-2 key words in `<em>` for accent color:
   "Plan your month at a <em>glance</em>"

6. **Test the scan.** Read ONLY the verbs across all screenshots:
   Plan → Plan → Reach → Add → Understand
   Do they tell a story? Would you download this app?

## Category Labels

Each screenshot gets a small category label above the headline (ALL CAPS):

```
MONTHLY PLANNING
YEARLY OVERVIEW
SAVINGS GOALS
QUICK ENTRY
MONTHLY DETAILS
PRIVACY
```

These provide context and help with ASO (App Store Optimization) keywords.
Keep them to 1-2 words, max 3.

## Subtitle Guidelines

Below the headline, a subtitle provides supporting detail. Rules:
- Max 2 lines
- Describe the specific feature that delivers the headline's benefit
- Use normal sentence case (not ALL CAPS)
- Can include specific numbers or details

```
Headline: "Plan your month at a glance"
Subtitle: "Income, expenses, and what's left over."

Headline: "Reach your savings goals"
Subtitle: "Visual, motivating — no bookkeeping."
```
